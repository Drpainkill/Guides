The Study

Your mouthwash is nuking your mouth.

A clinical study published in the Journal of Clinical Oral Investigations (2026) compared coconut oil pulling directly against chlorhexidine — the active ingredient in most prescription mouthwashes — in patients with active gum disease.

Chlorhexidine is what your dentist recommends. It kills bacteria. All of them. The bad ones, the good ones, everything living in your mouth gets scorched. No survivors.

"Coconut oil did the opposite. And it won."

  • Killed every major gum disease pathogen tested
  • Actively increased protective bacteria — chlorhexidine destroyed them
  • Maintained microbiome diversity — the standard treatment collapsed it
  • Outperformed the clinical gold standard on every gum health measure

The Science

Why coconut oil actually works.

Coconut oil is roughly 50% lauric acid — a medium-chain fatty acid that does something chlorhexidine cannot: it selectively destroys pathogenic bacteria while leaving beneficial ones completely intact.

Lauric acid disrupts bacterial cell membranes. Pathogenic bacteria — the ones destroying your gums — tend to be gram-negative and lipid-enveloped. These are specifically vulnerable. Commensal bacteria have different membrane structures and are largely spared.

"Think of it as a sniper versus a nuke. Chlorhexidine is the nuke. Coconut oil is the sniper."

The Protocol

How to actually do it.

The study used 5ml of coconut oil for 10 minutes per day. Here is exactly how to execute it.

1
Timing — Before anything else

Do it first thing in the morning, before eating, drinking, or brushing. Your mouth has been brewing bacteria all night. Pull before you introduce anything else.

2
Amount — One teaspoon

Roughly 5ml of coconut oil. Solid at room temperature, it melts within seconds in your mouth. Do not overthink this.

3
Swish — 10 minutes

Pull the oil through your teeth and around your gums for 10 minutes. Gentle pulling is enough. Work up to it if your jaw gets tired.

4
Spit — In the trash

Never in the sink. Coconut oil solidifies at room temperature and will clog your pipes. Never swallow it either — it has absorbed bacteria from your mouth.

5
Brush — After pulling

Rinse with warm water, then brush normally. Brushing after removes anything the oil loosened. This order matters.

Common Mistakes

Do not waste the 10 minutes.

✗
Swallowing the oil

It has absorbed the bacteria from your mouth. Swallowing it defeats the entire point and can upset your stomach.

✗
Spitting in the sink

Coconut oil solidifies at room temperature. Your pipes will hate you within a month.

✗
Doing it after brushing

Oil pulling before brushing loosens bacteria so you can flush and brush it away. Reversed order means you are just moving it around.

✗
Using a different oil

The clinical evidence is specifically for coconut oil and its lauric acid content. Sesame and sunflower oil lack the same antimicrobial profile.

✗
Stopping after a week

The study ran for a full month. Meaningful microbiome changes take time. Give it 30 days before judging results.
