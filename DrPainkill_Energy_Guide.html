The Problem

Your body isn't broken. It's running on garbage.

The exhaustion isn't a personality trait. It's not your age. It's not Monday. It's chemistry — and the chemistry running through your body right now is built to crash, not perform.

You're not tired because you're lazy. You're tired because you're slamming pure carbs at breakfast, drinking plain water on a depleted system, and then trying to caffeinate your way out of the hole. That isn't a strategy. That's a crisis on repeat.

Step 1 — Electrolytes

No sodium. No potassium. No power.

Your cells produce ATP — the energy currency of every single thing your body does. Move a finger, think a thought, digest lunch, blink. All ATP. And ATP does not get manufactured without three specific minerals doing the work at the cellular level: sodium, potassium, magnesium.

Most of you are running on fumes. You drink plain water all day, dilute the few electrolytes you have left, and then wonder why you feel like a wet sock by 2pm. The water isn't the problem. The water without minerals is the problem.

"Plain water on a depleted body is just dilution."

Add a pinch of salt to your morning water. Eat real food. Skip the pastel-colored sugar packets sold as "hydration."

Step 2 — Fat

Eat fat. Your brain is made of it.

Sixty percent of your brain is fat. Read that again. The organ you are using right now to process this sentence is more than half fat by composition. So when you cut fat out of your diet to "stay lean," you are starving the most metabolically demanding tissue in your entire body.

Fat burns clean and slow. No spike. No crash. No cortisol roller coaster. Just steady, hour-after-hour fuel that keeps your brain online and your hands working. Your ancestors didn't run on bagels. They ran on meat, organs, fish, nuts, and whatever fatty thing they could find — and their energy was stable because their fuel was stable.

"Sugar is sprint fuel. Fat is engine fuel."

Step 3 — Blood Sugar

Stop riding the goddamn rollercoaster.

Eating pure carbs for breakfast is a war crime against your afternoon. The bagel sends your insulin to the moon. You feel amazing for forty minutes — productive, sharp, awake. Then your blood sugar collapses and you spend the next two hours hunting for snacks like a raccoon in a dumpster.

The fix is not "no carbs." The fix is never carbs alone. Pair every carb with protein and fat, and your body stops getting on the ride in the first place. Slower digestion. Slower glucose release. Steady energy. No crash.

"You feel great for forty minutes. Then you crash like an unpaid intern."

1
Front-load protein and fat

Eggs, smoked salmon, full-fat yogurt, leftover steak. Anything that isn't pure starch. Your first meal sets the day's hormonal tone — make it boring on purpose.

2
Never eat a naked carb

Toast alone is a problem. Toast with butter, eggs, and avocado is a meal. Every time you reach for something starchy, ask what's pinning it down.

3
Walk after eating

Ten minutes after a meal lowers the glucose spike measurably. Costs zero dollars. Most people would rather sit and complain about their energy.

4
Vinegar before carb-heavy meals

A tablespoon of apple cider vinegar in water before a carb-heavy meal blunts the glucose response. Tastes terrible. Works anyway.

5
Save the carbs for after movement

Carbs after a workout get used by depleted muscle. Carbs after sitting on a couch get stored as fat and crash you out. Same food. Different outcome.

The Mistakes

Stop slamming the can.

Energy drinks work the same way being chased by a bear works. Cortisol up. Adrenaline up. Anxiety through the fucking roof. You feel sharp for an hour because your body thinks it's about to die. Then ninety minutes later you crash so hard you'd sell a kidney for a nap.

That isn't energy. That's borrowed panic. And you pay it back with interest every afternoon.

✗
Energy drinks before noon

Your cortisol is already peaking in the morning. Stacking caffeine on top is like flooring the gas with the parking brake on. You burn the engine, not the fuel.

✗
Coffee on an empty stomach

Spikes cortisol. Spikes anxiety. Wrecks your morning hormones. Eat protein and fat first, then drink the coffee. Same caffeine, completely different experience.

✗
Plain water all day with nothing else

You aren't hydrating. You're diluting. Salt your water. Eat real food. Hydration is a mineral problem, not a volume problem.

✗
Skipping fat to "stay lean"

Fat doesn't make you fat. Excess calories and blood sugar chaos make you fat. Cutting fat just guarantees you'll be hungry, foggy, and miserable.

✗
Treating sleep as optional

No protocol works on five hours of sleep. None of this. You can't biohack your way out of cumulative sleep debt. Go to bed.
